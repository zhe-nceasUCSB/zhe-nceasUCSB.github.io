<div style="margin-bottom: 2em;">
  <h3 style="margin-top: 0;"><a href="https://github.com/TitorX/SegMap-QGIS?tab=readme-ov-file#introduction" style="color: #2c3e50; text-decoration: none; border-bottom: 2px solid #3498db; padding-bottom: 2px; transition: color 0.3s ease;">SegMap - Interactive Segmentation Tool</a></h3>
  <div style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; margin-bottom: 1em;">
    <p style="margin: 0;">One of the major challenges in applying deep learning to remote sensing is the scarcity of precise training labels. To address this, I developed Segmap—a deep-learning-based interactive segmentation tool, now available as a QGIS plugin. SegMap enables rapid object extraction from aerial imagery with just a few clicks, providing up to 90% efficiency gains over traditional map digitization methods. Seamlessly integrated into QGIS, it streamlines land cover mapping, infrastructure monitoring, and environmental analysis by combining accurate AI-powered segmentation with intuitive user interaction, helping overcome the critical obstacle of limited training data in remote sensing.</p>

  <div style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start;">
    <img src="/images/heat-island.png" alt="Surface Urban Heat Islands" style="height: 200px; margin-right: 1.5em;">

  <h3 style="margin-top: 0;"><a href="https://www.nceas.ucsb.edu/monitoring-invasive-iceplant-space" style="color: #2c3e50; text-decoration: none; border-bottom: 2px solid #3498db; padding-bottom: 2px; transition: color 0.3s ease;">Monitoring Iceplant with AI</a></h3>
  <div style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start;">
    <img src="/images/iceplant.png" alt="Monitoring Iceplant with AI" style="height: 200px; margin-right: 1.5em;">
    <p style="margin: 0;">In a NASA-funded project, I developed a diffusion-based super-resolution workflow to upscale 3-m imagery to 0.6 m for monitoring iceplant from space. Using a deep learning-based interactive labeling tool I built, we created high-accuracy training data, and improved U-Net architecture to map iceplant in the Dangermond Preserve (2020–2025). The next goal is mapping iceplant along the entire California coast.</p>
